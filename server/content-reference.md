### Public Source: Exam English

Source:

- `https://www.examenglish.com/CAE/cae_listening3.htm`

Observed structure:

- Exam type: CAE Listening Part 3
- Difficulty: C1 / advanced
- Audio format: a conversation between two or more speakers
- Question format: 6 multiple choice questions
- Skill focus: understanding speakers' attitudes and opinions
- Playback rule: the audio can be listened to twice

Implications for generated material:

- Listening tasks should go beyond factual recall and test attitude, stance, implication, and opinion.
- Dialogue should sound natural and include disagreement, hesitation, clarification, contrast, or partial agreement.
- Options should be plausible and close enough to test advanced listening.
- Vocabulary should include attitude words, evaluative adjectives, stance phrases, and collocations used in discussions.

## Reusable Material Guidance

### Vocabulary Cards

Prefer words and phrases that are useful in real communication, such as:

- stance and opinion: `reservation`, `qualified agreement`, `point of view`, `implication`
- work and meetings: `agenda`, `follow-up`, `alignment`, `delegate`, `takeaway`
- travel and daily life: `itinerary`, `reservation`, `check-in`, `delay`, `flexible`
- discussion verbs: `argue`, `suggest`, `acknowledge`, `clarify`, `emphasize`

Vocabulary cards should emphasize:

- bilingual part of speech
- natural Chinese explanation
- collocations and scene-based usage
- easy-to-confuse neighboring words
- related clickable terms that can expand into new cards

### Reader Articles

Preferred article sources and styles:

- realistic news digest
- workplace trend summary
- technology and product updates
- lifestyle or travel articles grounded in real-world situations

Reader articles should:

- feel current and realistic
- avoid childish textbook stories
- include a clear key word and a representative sentence
- support discussion prompts after reading
- show a visible update time in the product

### Listening And Assessment

Reference style for advanced listening:

- use two or more speakers when suitable
- ask about attitude, purpose, concern, hidden preference, or interpretation
- keep answer choices close and realistic
- allow a second listen in product design when appropriate

### Talk With Me

Conversation prompts may borrow:

- workplace meeting discussions
- travel planning and changes
- opinion exchange around a short article
- follow-up questions that ask the learner to explain why, compare options, or express agreement with limits

#### Scenario 4.1: Describing Data & Trends
